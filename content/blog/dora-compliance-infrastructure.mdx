## The DORA Imperative

The Digital Operational Resilience Act (DORA) — Regulation (EU) 2022/2554 — represents a paradigm shift in how the European financial sector manages ICT risk. Effective from January 2025, it mandates that financial entities establish comprehensive frameworks for ICT risk management, incident reporting, resilience testing, and third-party oversight.

For infrastructure architects, DORA isn't just a compliance checkbox — it's a fundamental redesign of how we think about operational resilience.

## Five Pillars, One Architecture

DORA's requirements map directly to infrastructure architecture decisions:

### 1. ICT Risk Management Framework

Your infrastructure must support continuous asset discovery and dependency mapping. This means:

- **Automated CMDB** with real-time topology updates
- **Dependency graphs** that trace service chains from user-facing applications to bare-metal
- **Risk scoring** integrated into change management workflows

```yaml
# Example: Ansible inventory with risk classification
all:
  children:
    critical_tier1:
      vars:
        dora_risk_class: "critical"
        rto_hours: 4
        rpo_hours: 1
      hosts:
        core-db-01:
          function: "Primary transaction database"
        auth-server-01:
          function: "Identity provider"
```

### 2. Incident Classification & Reporting

DORA requires major ICT incidents to be reported to competent authorities within strict timeframes. Your monitoring stack must support:

- **Automated severity classification** based on impact metrics
- **Regulatory reporting workflows** with 4-hour initial notification SLAs
- **Root cause analysis** with full audit trails

### 3. Digital Operational Resilience Testing

Regular testing — including threat-led penetration testing (TLPT) — must be embedded into operations:

- **Automated DR failover tests** with measured RTO/RPO
- **Chaos engineering** for critical service paths
- **Red team exercises** against production-equivalent environments

### 4. Third-Party ICT Risk

Critical service providers must be monitored continuously:

- **Concentration risk analysis** for cloud and managed service dependencies
- **Exit strategies** with tested migration runbooks for every critical provider
- **Contractual audit rights** enabling infrastructure inspections

### 5. Information Sharing

Participation in cyber threat intelligence sharing arrangements is encouraged:

- **SIEM integration** with industry threat feeds
- **Automated indicator correlation** for proactive defense
- **Anonymized incident data** sharing with sector peers

## Architecture Patterns for DORA

The most effective DORA-compliant architectures share common patterns:

**Active-Active Multi-Site**: Eliminates single points of failure and enables sub-4-hour RTO through automatic failover.

**Immutable Infrastructure**: Infrastructure as Code ensures reproducibility and auditability — every change is versioned, reviewed, and traceable.

**Zero-Trust Segmentation**: Microsegmented networks limit blast radius and provide granular audit trails required for incident investigation.
